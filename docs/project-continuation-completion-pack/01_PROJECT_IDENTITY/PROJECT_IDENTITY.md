# Project Identity

## Project name
Evida / CasePilot.

## One-line description
A desktop-first, local legal workspace for document import, source-bound case overview, Saksrom dialogue and controlled legal workrooms.

## What the project is supposed to be
Evida is intended to become a trustworthy legal work surface where documents are imported locally, converted into traceable source objects, used for grounded case summaries and legal Q&A, then routed into workrooms such as chronology, evidence, arguments, contradictions, risk and litigation simulation.

## Current perceived stage
Pre-alpha technical scaffold with a working Tauri desktop evaluation build. It is not production-ready.

## Who the project is for
Legal professionals and evaluators who need a local-first workflow for exploring case documents and generating source-bound preliminary understanding. Current use should be limited to synthetic, sample or approved evaluation material.

## Commercial or internal purpose
The likely commercial direction is a premium legal desktop tool. Current practical purpose is evaluation, technical validation and product shaping.

## Original goal
Build a legal AI/document workspace that can ingest documents, preserve source traceability, support legal reasoning workrooms and keep sensitive legal data under strict control.

## Current goal now
Stabilize the local evaluation build, clarify production boundaries, remove ambiguous legacy ownership, harden security/data controls, and turn the current useful prototype into a testable MVP.

## What changed over time
The repo contains multiple phases, legacy scaffolds and old backend starts. The canonical direction is now clearer: Tauri desktop for local app experience and Spring Boot for future enterprise/control-plane ownership. FastAPI starter material is deprecated for enterprise/control-plane responsibilities.

## Why this pack is being used now
The repository has enough working product surface to continue, but also enough docs, legacy folders and planned-vs-real ambiguity that a continuation pack is needed to prevent drift.
