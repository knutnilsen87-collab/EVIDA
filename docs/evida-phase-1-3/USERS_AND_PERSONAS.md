# USERS_AND_PERSONAS

## Primary users
Case owners who are responsible for moving individual cases forward.

## Secondary users
Managers, reviewers, admins, and collaborators who need visibility, approval, reporting, or handoff context.

## Persona 1: Case owner
- Handles multiple active cases.
- Needs fast intake, clear current state, next actions, notes, file context, and reminders.
- Pain points: context switching, scattered information, missed follow-ups, unclear ownership.
- Uses Evida to know what matters now.

## Persona 2: Team lead / manager
- Oversees many cases and people.
- Needs status visibility, risk flags, workload view, and unresolved blockers.
- Pain points: status meetings, hidden delays, inconsistent reporting, limited auditability.
- Uses Evida to see portfolio health and intervene early.

## Persona 3: Reviewer / specialist
- Provides input on specific cases.
- Needs concise history, relevant files, questions needing review, and decision context.
- Pain points: being pulled in late with no context.
- Uses Evida to understand the case quickly and leave traceable input.

## Persona 4: Admin / operator
- Manages users, templates, statuses, workflows, and permissions.
- Needs stable controls, logs, exports, and safe recovery paths.
- Pain points: messy data, accidental deletion, permission mistakes.
- Uses Evida to keep the workspace reliable.

## Roles/access model
- Owner: owns cases, invites collaborators, edits case details.
- Collaborator: contributes notes/tasks/files to assigned cases.
- Reviewer: comments, reviews AI outputs, approves decisions if workflow requires it.
- Manager: sees team-level dashboards and all cases within a workspace.
- Admin: manages workspace settings, users, roles, templates, exports, and retention.
