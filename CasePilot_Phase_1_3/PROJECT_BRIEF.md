# PROJECT_BRIEF

## Project name
CasePilot

## One-line summary
CasePilot helps teams intake, organize, analyze, and progress cases with an AI-assisted workspace.

## Problem the project solves
Many teams handle cases across email, documents, chat, spreadsheets, CRM notes, and ad hoc folders. Important context gets fragmented, follow-ups are missed, and new team members need too much time to understand the current state of a case.

CasePilot should provide one structured place for case context, tasks, evidence/files, notes, decisions, and AI-assisted summaries.

## Who the project is for
- Small and medium professional teams handling recurring cases or client matters.
- Operators, consultants, legal-adjacent teams, support teams, compliance teams, agencies, and internal admin teams.
- Founders or managers who need visibility into case progress without micromanaging every detail.

## Why this project should exist
The value is not just storing cases. The value is reducing cognitive load: turning messy information into a clear current-state view, recommended next actions, and traceable decisions.

## What success looks like
- A user can create a case in under 2 minutes.
- A user can see the status, owner, risk level, next action, and latest summary of each case.
- AI can summarize case history and propose next steps while clearly separating facts from suggestions.
- Team members can collaborate without losing traceability.
- The product is safe enough for real work: auth, permissions, audit trail, backups, logging, and clear data handling.

## Long-term vision
CasePilot becomes an operational command center for case-heavy work: intake, structured reasoning, document support, collaboration, workflow automation, and reporting.

## Non-goals for v1
- Not a full CRM.
- Not a full legal practice management system.
- Not a generic project management app.
- Not an autonomous agent that makes final decisions without user confirmation.
- Not a replacement for domain experts.

## Geographic/language scope
Initial product can support English and Norwegian UI/content assumptions, but MVP should be built language-neutral enough to add localization later.

## Business/commercial context
Likely SaaS with team-based subscriptions. MVP can start without billing if the first goal is pilot validation.

## Known constraints
- Product scope must stay narrow in v1.
- AI output must be traceable, reviewable, and non-authoritative.
- Sensitive data handling must be designed early.
- Exact industry/domain is not yet confirmed.
